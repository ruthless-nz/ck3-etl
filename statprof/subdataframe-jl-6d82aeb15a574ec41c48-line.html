1 """
2 SubDataFrame{<:AbstractDataFrame,<:AbstractIndex,<:AbstractVector{Int}} <: AbstractDataFrame
3
4 A view of an `AbstractDataFrame`. It is returned by a call to the `view` function
5 on an `AbstractDataFrame` if a collections of rows and columns are specified.
6
7 A `SubDataFrame` is an `AbstractDataFrame`, so expect that most
8 DataFrame functions should work. Such methods include `describe`,
9 `summary`, `nrow`, `size`, `by`, `stack`, and `join`.
10
11 If the selection of columns in a parent data frame is passed as `:` (a colon)
12 then `SubDataFrame` will always have all columns from the parent,
13 even if they are added or removed after its creation.
14
15 # Examples
16 ```julia
17 df = DataFrame(a = repeat([1, 2, 3, 4], outer=[2]),
18 b = repeat([2, 1], outer=[4]),
19 c = randn(8))
20 sdf1 = view(df, 2:3) # column subsetting
21 sdf2 = @view df[end:-1:1, [1,3]] # row and column subsetting
22 sdf3 = groupby(df, :a)[1] # indexing a GroupedDataFrame returns a SubDataFrame
23 ```
